## CUTTING SEMANTIC NETWORK: 乂 and Related Variants

### Core Insight: Multiple Graph Forms, Single Semantic

**The cutting semantic is expressed through multiple related forms:**
- **乂** (crossed cutting blades - scissors/shears) - CORE PRIMITIVE
- **刀** (single blade knife) - DERIVED TOOL
- **刃** (blade edge with marker) - BLADE CONCEPT
- **匕** (spoon/dagger - curved blade) - SPECIALIZED TOOL
- **刂** (standing knife radical - 立刀旁) - VARIANT POSITION
- **⺈** (knife variant) - VARIANT FORM

**Semantic network approach recognizes:**
- All encode CUTTING concept from different angles
- Different tool types: scissors (乂), knife (刀), blade (刃), dagger (匕)
- Positional variants: 刀 (full form) vs. 刂 (radical form on right)
- **乂 is the PRIMITIVE** (crossed blades = earliest cutting tool concept)
- Others are DERIVED or SPECIALIZED forms

This section focuses on **乂 (crossed cutting blades)** as the core primitive, but recognizes its relationship to the broader cutting semantic network.

### Why 乂 is the PRIMITIVE

**1. Mechanical sophistication:**
- Crossed blades = leverage/mechanical advantage
- Single blade (刀) = simpler, direct force
- Crossing is MORE FUNDAMENTAL concept

**2. Philosophical depth:**
- 爻 (I Ching) = doubled 乂 = transformation principle
- Crossing = interaction of forces → change
- 刀 lacks this philosophical extension

**3. Civilizational impact:**
- **文 (writing)** derives from 乂 (crossed patterns/carving)
- Writing = foundation of civilization
- 刀 enables carving but 乂 encodes the CONCEPT

**4. Semantic breadth:**
- 乂: cutting → governing → transformation → writing → civilization
- 刀: cutting → carving → punishment (narrower range)

**5. Conceptual priority:**
- Crossing = fundamental operation (intersection, combination)
- Single blade = particular instance
- 乂 operates at higher level of abstraction
