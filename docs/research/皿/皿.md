## TRADITIONAL INTERPRETATION

**Common meaning:** Vessel, dish, container, plate

**Kangxi radical #108:** One of the 214 traditional radicals

**Traditional understanding:**
- Pictographic representation of a vessel/dish
- Shows a container viewed from above or side
- Associated with containers, holding, containing

## PRELIMINARY SEMANTIC ANALYSIS

### Pattern 1: **Literal Containers/Vessels**

**Direct vessel meanings:**
- **盆** (basin) - washing basin, large bowl
- **盘** (plate/dish) - flat serving vessel
- **盏** (cup) - small drinking vessel
- **盅** (small cup) - wine cup
- **盒** (box) - container with lid
- **盂** (bowl) - round vessel
- **盎** (pot/bowl) - cooking/storage vessel
- **盔** (helmet) - vessel that contains/protects the head

**Consistency:** High - many characters directly mean containers

### Pattern 2: **Containing/Holding Actions**

**Verbs related to vessels:**
- **盛** (contain, hold, ladle) - putting things in vessel
- **盈** (full, filled) - state of vessel being full
- **盖** (cover, lid) - covering a vessel
- **盥** (wash in basin) - using vessel for washing

**Observation:** Actions associated with using vessels

### Pattern 3: **Fullness/Abundance/Surplus**

**Abundance concepts:**
- **盈** (full, surplus, profit) - vessel overflowing
- **益** (benefit, increase, profit) - overflow benefits
- **盛** (prosperous, flourishing) - fullness metaphor
- **盎** (abundant) - vessel filled abundantly

**Metaphorical extension:** Full vessel → prosperity/abundance

### Pattern 4: **Supervising/Monitoring**

**Supervision:**
- **监** (supervise, monitor, prison) - watching over, oversight

**Question:** Why does 监 (supervise) contain 皿 (vessel)?

### Pattern 5: **Alliance/Oath/Ritual**

**Ceremonial usage:**
- **盟** (alliance, oath, covenant) - blood oath ceremony?
- **盉** (ancient ritual wine vessel) - ceremonial vessel

**Possible connection:** Vessels used in oath/alliance rituals?

### Pattern 6: **Salt and Preservation**

**Special case:**
- **盐** (salt) = 卤 (brine) + 皿 (vessel)
- Salt made/stored in vessels
- Or: brine in vessel → salt production

### Pattern 7: **Theft/Taking**

**Negative action:**
- **盗** (steal, rob, thief) - taking from vessel?
- Stealing food/valuables from containers?

## WORKING HYPOTHESES

### Hypothesis 1: "Container/Vessel (Literal)"

**Traditional view - mostly correct for basic meaning**

**Evidence:**
- Many characters are literal vessels (盆, 盘, 盏, 盅, 盒, 盂, 盎)
- 皿 visually represents a dish/bowl
- Straightforward pictographic meaning

**Challenges:**
- Doesn't explain 监 (supervise)
- Why 盗 (steal)?
- Why 盟 (alliance/oath)?

### Hypothesis 2: "Ritual/Ceremonial Vessel"

**Beyond simple container - ceremonial significance**

**Evidence:**
- **盟** (alliance, oath) - blood oath ceremony using vessel?
- **盉** (ritual wine vessel) - explicit ceremonial use
- **盐** (salt) - used in purification rituals?
- Ancient Chinese rituals involved vessel offerings

**Supporting theory:**
- Vessels were central to ritual practices
- Blood oaths sealed in vessels (盟)
- Offerings placed in vessels
- Supervising (监) may relate to ritual oversight?

**Archaeological context:**
- Bronze Age China: elaborate ritual vessels (鼎, 簋, etc.)
- 皿 may specifically denote ritual importance

### Hypothesis 3: "Supervising/Overseeing"

**Explaining 监 (supervise):**

**Possible explanations:**
- **监** (supervise) - overseeing vessels/containers?
- Ritual supervision of sacred vessels?
- Supervising storage/distribution of resources in vessels?
- Prison (监狱) as place where people are "contained" like vessels?

**Archaeological evidence needed:**
- Role of vessel oversight in ancient China
- Connection between supervision and container management?

### Hypothesis 4: "Measuring/Standard/Reference"

**Vessels as measurement tools:**

**Evidence:**
- **盈** (full) - measured fullness
- **益** (benefit, increase) - measured overflow
- **盛** (contain) - measured capacity
- **盟** (covenant) - standard agreement?

**Ancient practice:**
- Vessels used to measure grain, liquids
- Standard volumes for trade
- Fair measurement = justice/supervision (监)?

### Hypothesis 5: "Gathering/Collecting/Assembling"

**Vessel as gathering place:**

**Evidence:**
- **盟** (alliance) - gathering of parties in covenant
- **盗** (steal) - gathering/taking what's not yours
- **盆** (basin) - gathers water
- **盈** (full) - gathered to fullness
- **监** (supervise) - gathering under oversight
