## Research Notes

### On the "Especially/Particularly" Meaning

The usage of 尤 as "especially/particularly" (尤其, 尤为) is extremely common in modern Chinese, but how does this relate to original "bitter feeling" or "fault/blame"?

**Possible evolution:**
1. Bitter feeling → Strong feeling/emotion → Emphasis/intensity → "Especially"
2. Deviation/abnormality → Standing out → Prominence → "Especially"
3. Fault/blame → Pointing out → Singling out → "Especially/particularly"

### On Traditional Etymology

Traditional sources often gloss 尤 as:
- 異也 (different, unusual)
- 過也 (fault, error)
- 怨也 (resentment)
- 甚也 (extreme, very)

This suggests the "deviation/irregularity/extremity" interpretation may have traditional support.
