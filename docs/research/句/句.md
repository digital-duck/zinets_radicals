## 1. PRELIMINARY ANALYSIS

### Traditional Interpretation
- **Modern meanings:**
  1. 句 (jù) = sentence, verse, phrase
  2. 句 (gōu) = bend, curved
- **Traditional etymology:** Often described as mouth + hook/bend
- **Kangxi status:** Radical #30 variant

### Character Structure
- **Oracle bone forms:** 2 attestations (J04770-J04771)
  - VERY LIMITED oracle bone evidence
  - Shows hook or curved form
- **Bronze inscriptions:** 14 attestations (B02820-B02833)
  - More developed forms showing hook/curved structure
- **Seal script:** 1 form (S01627)
- **Liushutong:** 6 variant forms (L12133-L20431)

### Oracle Bone Visual Analysis

**Critical observation:** Only 2 oracle bone forms!
- Shows hook or curved bend shape
- Mouth-like opening at one end
- Hook/bend structure clearly depicted

**Components visible:**
- 勹 (bāo) = embrace, wrap around
- 口 (kǒu) = mouth, opening

**Traditional decomposition:** 勹 + 口 = 句

### Initial Observations

The oracle/bronze forms show:
1. **Hook or curved shape** - consistent across all forms
2. **Opening/mouth element** - at one end of curve
3. **Bending/embracing structure** - wrapping around

**Modern meanings seem disparate:**
- "Sentence/phrase" (abstract linguistic)
- "Bend/curved" (physical shape)

**Challenge:** How do these connect? What's the original concrete meaning?

## 4. THE DOG (狗) CONNECTION - Critical Analysis

### Why 犭 + 句 = Dog Specifically?

**This is the KEY PUZZLE to solve!**

**Possibility 1: Dog's Physical Form**
- Curved/hooked tail (many dog breeds) ✓
- Bent posture when crouching or stalking
- Curved spine when running

**Possibility 2: Dog's Behavior**
- "Hooking" or catching prey with mouth
- Grasping with jaws (hook-like action)
- Bending/curved movement while hunting

**Possibility 3: Ancient Dog Depiction**
- Perhaps 句 originally was a specific dog pictograph?
- Later abstracted to mean "hook/curve" generally?
- **BUT:** Oracle bone form doesn't look dog-like

**Possibility 4: Phonetic Component**
- 句 (gōu) close to 狗 (gǒu)
- Primarily phonetic usage in this character?
- **BUT:** Why choose 句 phonetically if no semantic connection?

**Most likely:** Dogs have notably curved/hooked tails compared to wolves. The tail curl is a domestication feature. 犭 + 句 (curved/hooked) = domestic dog with curled tail.

## 6. THE SENTENCE/PHRASE CONNECTION

### How Did 句 Come to Mean "Sentence"?

**句 (jù) = sentence, phrase, verse**

**Possible explanations:**

**Hypothesis 1: Measured Unit (like a hook)**
- Hook = defined length/unit
- Sentence = measured unit of speech
- Both are "bounded/defined portions"
- 局 (bounded area) shares this "defined space" semantic

**Hypothesis 2: Bending/Turning Point**
- Sentence = where speech bends/pauses
- Hook = where line bends
- Grammatical unit = turning point in meaning

**Hypothesis 3: Hooked/Linked Units**
- Sentences hook together to form discourse
- Like hooks linking a chain
- Grasping/connecting meaning units

**Hypothesis 4: Phonetic Borrowing**
- Originally meant "hook/curve"
- Borrowed for abstract "sentence" meaning
- Semantic bleaching over time

**Most likely:** Combination of 1 & 4 - measured unit (like hook length) borrowed for linguistic units, with some metaphorical connection to "grasping meaning."

**Hooks are fundamental tools:**
1. **Fishhooks:** Essential fishing technology (笱 fish trap)

3. **Agricultural hooks:** Harvesting, pulling, clearing

**Hook innovation:**
- Curved barb prevents escape (catching function)
- Converts straight pull into gripping force
- Simple but highly effective design

#### Observable in Nature

**Natural hooks/curves:**
- Plant tendrils (curved grasping)
- Animal claws (hooked for catching)
- Curved branches used as tools
- Dog's curved tail (domestication marker)

### Cultural Salience

**Why hooks important:**
- **Fishing:** Critical protein source (笱 trap technology)
- **Hunting:** Grappling, securing prey
- **Agriculture:** Harvesting tools
- **Daily life:** Fastening, hanging, securing

**Embodied knowledge:**
- Curved vs. straight = functional difference
- Hook shape = catching/grasping capability
- Bending = creating enclosed/secured space

## 9. BREAKTHROUGH REINTERPRETATION

### Taking It At Face Value: 句 = SPOKEN MESSAGE/UTTERANCE

**Shuowen says:** "曲也從口" (curved, from mouth)
- NOT just "sound from mouth"
- But **MESSAGE that comes out of mouth**
- **COMMUNICATION, UTTERANCE, SPOKEN EXPRESSION**

### Oracle Bone Evidence Reexamined

**The form shows:**
- 口 (mouth/opening) clearly depicted
- Curved/bent element = **something coming OUT of mouth**
- NOT a fishhook - it's **SPEECH emerging from mouth!**

**Visual metaphor:**
- Speech curves/bends as it emerges from mouth
- Communication that wraps around (勹 embrace + 口 mouth)
- Utterance = bounded unit of speech

### Core Meaning REVISED

**句 (jù) = SPOKEN MESSAGE, UTTERANCE, COMMUNICATION**

**Original meaning:** A unit of spoken communication - what emerges from the mouth

**Why "curved/bent"?**
- Speech bends and turns (not straight)
- Communication wraps around meaning
- Utterance has boundaries (beginning and end)

**Primary semantic contribution:**
1. **Communication:** Spoken message, utterance
2. **Bounded unit:** Defined portion of speech (sentence, phrase)
3. **Expression:** What comes out of mouth
4. **Message:** Attempting to convey meaning

### THE DOG (狗) CONNECTION - COMPLETELY SOLVED! ✓✓✓

**狗 (gǒu) = 犭 + 句 = DOG**

**Why animal + speech/utterance = DOG?**

**Because dogs are THE COMMUNICATING ANIMAL!**
- Dogs bark constantly trying to communicate with humans
- Dogs vocalize more than any other domestic animal
- Dogs "speak" but humans don't understand their message
- **The animal that TRIES TO TALK but can't form human speech!**

**This is PROFOUND:**
- 犭 (animal) + 句 (utterance/message) = the animal that attempts speech
- Dogs are uniquely vocal among domesticated animals
- Constant barking, whining, howling = attempted communication
- We hear the sounds (口) but don't get the message (句)

**Cultural observation:**
- Humans recognized dogs' unique communicative behavior
- Among all animals, dogs vocalize TOWARD humans specifically
- This is a behavioral observation of domestication!
- **狗 = "the barking animal," "the animal that tries to speak"**
