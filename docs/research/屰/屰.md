## 5. MATERIAL CULTURE CONTEXT: Why "Going Backwards" Matters

### 5.1 Physical Reversal in Neolithic/Bronze Age Life

**When does going backwards/contrary direction matter?**

1. **Hunting/warfare:**
   - Retreat vs. advance
   - Enemy approaching from behind (contrary direction)
   - Flanking maneuvers (attacking from opposite side)

2. **River navigation:**
   - Going upstream vs. downstream
   - Against current = hard, contrary work
   - 逆流 (nìliú) = go against current (still used today!)

3. **Social order:**
   - Obedience vs. disobedience (child obeying parent vs. 逆子 rebellious son)
   - Following vs. opposing authority
   - **Hierarchical society** needs concept of "going against" authority

4. **Astronomical/calendrical:**
   - Moon cycle reversal (new moon = turning point)
   - Seasonal changes (solstice = reversal point)
   - 朔 (new moon) = beginning of new cycle

### 5.2 Why This Deserves Encoding as Primitive

**Cognitive/cultural importance:**
- **Directional opposition** is fundamental category
- Forward/backward, obey/rebel, with/against = binary oppositions
- **Spatial orientation** extended to social/moral domain
- 12 oracle forms = concept important enough to encode frequently

**Semantic productivity:**
- Though network small, meanings are **fundamental**
- 逆 (oppose, rebel) = extremely common, important character
- Extends from physical (backwards) → social (disobedient) → moral (rebellious)

## 7. THE 朔 PUZZLE: Deep Dive

### 7.1 Why Does 朔 (New Moon) Contain 屰 (Backwards/Reversal)?

**朔 = 屰 + 月 (moon)**

**Astronomical interpretation (STRONG):**

**Lunar cycle as reversal process:**
1. **Waxing phase:** New moon → crescent → first quarter → gibbous → full moon
2. **Waning phase:** Full moon → gibbous → last quarter → crescent → **new moon (朔)**
3. **New moon (朔) = REVERSAL POINT** - moon "goes backwards" from visible → invisible → visible again

**屰 (reversal) + 月 (moon) = 朔 (reversal point in lunar cycle)** ✓✓✓

**This is BRILLIANT semantic composition:**
- Not just "first day" arbitrarily
- **Marks the reversal/turning point** in monthly cycle
- Moon disappears (reverses from visibility) then reappears
- Ancient astronomers encoded **cyclical reversal** concept

### 7.2 朔 = North (Secondary Meaning)

**Why does 朔 also mean "north"?**

**Directional symbolism:**
- **South** (南) = emperor's direction, forward, yang, light
- **North** (北) = contrary/opposite direction, yin, dark
- 朔方 (shuòfāng) = the north = **contrary/opposite region**

**屰 (contrary) + 月 → extended to contrary direction = north**

**Or possibly:**
- New moon (朔) appears in northern sky in certain seasons
- Astronomical observation → directional association

### 7.3 Coherence Check: Does 朔 Fit?

**YES - 朔 fits "reversal/contrary" semantic perfectly:**
- New moon = **reversal point** in cycle ✓✓✓
- North = **contrary direction** to south ✓✓✓
- Both meanings derive from 屰 (reversal/contrary) ✓✓✓

**Revised coherence: 100%** - all characters in network transparent!
