### Critical Observations

1. **祖 (ancestor) seems CENTRAL**:
   - 礻(altar/spirit) + 且
   - If 且 relates to ancestors/altars, this is semantically transparent
   - Traditional interpretation: 且 depicts altar or offering stand
   - Need oracle bone evidence to verify

2. **Possible "stacked/layered" structure**:
   - 且 character itself shows horizontal lines stacked
   - Could represent: stacked offerings? Stacked altar tiers? Layers?
   - 组 (organize) = arranging in stacks/layers
   - 阻 (block) = layered obstruction

3. **"Coarse/rough" pattern is interesting**:
   - 粗 (coarse rice/grain)
   - 苴 (coarse hemp cloth)
   - Could 且 relate to **inferior/coarse quality**?
   - Or **unsorted/unrefined** (not yet organized)?

4. **姐 (older sister) is puzzling**:
   - Why does 女 + 且 = older sister specifically?
   - Does 且 imply **precedence, seniority, rank**?
   - Like 祖 (ancestor) = one who comes before/first?

5. **The negative cluster needs explanation**:
   - Many characters have negative/problematic meanings
   - 沮 (dejected), 怚 (anxious), 疽 (abscess), 蛆 (maggot), 殂 (die)
   - Does 且 have negative connotation?
   - Or purely phonetic for these?
