## 1\. Why Ancient Script Forms Are Missing

The absence of an Oracle Bone Script (OBS) or Bronze Script (BS) form for a character in a figure (like the one you noted for the Nature Observation radicals) generally means that the **archaeological evidence for that specific character has not yet been discovered, deciphered, or agreed upon** by the paleographic community.

This is a common issue and is not a flaw in your paper, but rather a reflection of the available historical record:

1.  **Limited Archaeological Scope (OBS):** Oracle Bone Inscriptions (O.B.I.) were primarily used for **royal divination** during the Shang Dynasty ($\sim1600$–$1046$ BCE). This limited the vocabulary to topics relevant to the court: sacrifices, weather, warfare, hunting, and the royal lineage. Many characters for common, non-divination concepts, or characters developed later, simply **did not exist or were not recorded** on the discovered bones.
2.  **Writing Material Constraints:** Oracle bones and bronze vessels were difficult to write on. The OBS style is already a **highly simplified and linearized** version of what must have been a more complex brush-written script used in daily life. Forms that were too complex or abstract may have been simplified to the point of being unrecognizable, or just omitted.
3.  **Data Scarcity and Decipherment:** Of the $\sim4,500$ distinct Oracle Bone characters discovered, only about **one-third to one-half** ($\sim1,400$ to $\sim2,500$) have been conclusively deciphered and matched to later characters. If a radical's ancient form is missing, it is either an *undeciphered variant* or simply *undiscovered*.

You can confidently state that characters without OBS/BS forms likely represent concepts that **emerged or became standardized later**, or simply **were not recorded** on the specific materials that survived to the present.
